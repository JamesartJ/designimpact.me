My First Steps

When I was four I kept a notebook of inventions. Under the monicker Inventor Jaime Gadget, I dreamed of a future, sketched out in heavy black pencil, where everything could fly. Elementary school lunches were spent pouring over Star Wars cross sections as I searched for answers to the mysteries of designing lightsabers and X-wings. I wanted to build the mythical and magical, creations unconstrained by what I was taught possible. So I sketched diagrams and labeled the measurements and specifications, envisioning how to bring these dreams to life.

The Real World

After finishing my coursework early in the fall of 2016, I began working for a startup in Oakland as a product designer. After a successful product launch, I left for India to volunteer at a summer camp in Janwar, Madhya Pradesh. Taking inspiration from Skateistan, a German woman Ulrike built a skate park there which has encouraged community development and academic participation. I taught music and english, and for the first time really saw how powerful an impact simple changes in infrastructure can have on a community.

New Horizons

I returned with a renewed sense in the power of design to create an impact, and to continue my growth joined the Agency Swirl in the Presidio. While there, I worked as a UX designer on products for Microsoft, American Express, Juniper Networks, and others. I have since joined Facebook as a product designer on their bizapps team, designing software for use across internal supply chains. While my work has allowed for an improvement in the experience of many users, the scope has been narrow. Design can go beyond a human centered approach to a humanity centered one. I plan to use of these skills, and the many more to learn, in order to tackle the problems that affect us all globally, to design impact.
